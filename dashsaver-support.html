DashSaver — Clocks, Weather & Live Stocks

Need help with DashSaver? Browse the common questions below or reach out directly — we typically respond within 1–2 business days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I launch DashSaver?

Find DashSaver on your Roku home screen and press OK to launch it, just like any other channel. The dashboard will appear immediately with live clocks, weather, and stocks.

How do I change the cities, time zones, or stock symbols?

While DashSaver is open, press the ★ (Options) button on your Roku remote to open the settings screen. From there you can customize all clocks, weather cities, and stock symbols. Your changes are saved automatically and will be there next time you launch the app.

How do I exit DashSaver?

Press the Back button on your Roku remote at any time to exit and return to the Roku home screen.

Why isn't the weather showing?

DashSaver needs an internet connection to fetch weather data. Make sure your Roku is connected to Wi-Fi and try closing and relaunching the app. If the issue persists, the weather service may be temporarily unavailable — data retries automatically every 10 minutes.

Why aren't the stock prices updating?

Stock data is refreshed every 10 minutes. Prices are only available during market hours (weekdays, 9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET). Outside market hours, the last known price is displayed.

My settings were lost — what happened?

Settings are stored on your Roku device and persist across restarts and app relaunches. If a factory reset was performed on the device, settings will be cleared. You can re-enter them by pressing ★ Options inside the app.

Is DashSaver free?

Yes. DashSaver is completely free with no subscriptions, no in-app purchases, and no ads — ever.

Which Roku devices are supported?

DashSaver supports HD and FHD Roku devices running firmware 15.1 or later. It has been tested on Roku streaming sticks and Roku TV models.

Contact Support

If your issue isn't covered above, please reach out and include:

  • Your Roku model and firmware version (found in Settings → System → About)
  • A description of what you expected to happen vs. what actually happened
  • Any error messages displayed on screen
